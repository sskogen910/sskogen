Getting Started

Getting started is always the hardest part of any new hobby, and gardening is no exception! Here are a few ways to help you get started that I have found over the years.

Tips For Getting Started

One of the best ways to get started is by finding good resources. Gardening books, such as these thrift store finds, or books from a library that can be found on apps like Libby can help you learn the basics.

Mulch is a great tool to use in a garden, and in most instances you can get mulch for free by contacting your local tree services, power companies, or municipals.

They are likely very happy to dump mulched trees from clearing projects on your property instead of having to pay someone to dispose of chipped trees.

You can find large plots in many places; online marketplaces are a great way to find gently used pots at a deep discount. Sometimes you can get great deals on pots at a store at the end of season to have for the next season.

It is also OK to use large buckets when in a pinch just make sure there's plenty of drainage.

Sharing plants with friends or getting plants to propagate from friends is another great way to save money on increasing the number of plants you have.
