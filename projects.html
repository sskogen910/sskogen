Projects

I moved into this house in 2016, so I’ve been here about 10 years now, and my garden space has changed a lot during that time. In the beginning, there was no fence, but as my ambition grew, the fence did too—slowly extending further down the side of the house to include more space. I’ve used the garden in different ways and experimented with various methods I’ve learned along the way—not always with great success, but every failed attempt has taught me something.

Strawberry Planter Rebuild

I originally planted strawberries in two white wooden planters about three years ago, recently I needed to replace them.

I dug up the strawberries and temporarily moved them into smaller pots while I rebuilt the space.

Over time they had rotted and were badly damaged by termites, so I demoed them.

I laid down cardboard, set up a galvanized raised planter, lined it with landscaping fabric, and filled it with fresh soil. After replanting the strawberries, I added fencing to keep chickens and other animals out while the plants recovered.

Front Garden Space

This space is the front portion of my garden, a large stretch of dirt surrounded by concrete. It is in the front of my house in early 2016, when I first moved in and had not done much yet.

Over the past 10 years, I’ve used it in a variety of ways, including growing tomatoes and other plants.

I have tried different techniques of growing plants and have found success at times.

There have been times it was not as successful, and in the failing I learned new things.

My chickens help to keep it bug free, but the plants can get unruly.

The fence was added within the first few years, but has changed configuration and style a few times.

How I’ve used the space has changed over time as I’ve experimented and adapted.

Mulch has been a game changer.

Patio

The patio in the beginning only had a table and chairs. It sits between the front and back portions of my garden.

Along the back edge of the patio, there’s about a one-foot gap between the concrete and the house. When I first moved in, I spent days digging out invasive roots.

I have since put a lot of time into planting flowers there.

Right now, that space includes several varieties of amaryllis, gladiolus, hyacinths, tulips, crocus, daffodils, irises, and many other flowers I’ve collected over the years.

I currently have 8 varieties of amaryllis. A friend gave me these, and they’ve spread so much that I’ve dug some up and shared them with others.

When there is limited space there are many ways to maximize the number of plants by going vertical.

Over the years I have found large pots in many ways, some in local online marketplaces, some on sale in stores, I have even used large buckets with holes drilled.

Container gardening is a great option when there is limited space or if you rent.

Container gardening is also great for those with disabilites because they do not need to be on the ground.

Back Garden Space

The back portion of my garden was not a part of the original plans but a few years in I needed more space and I expanded.

In the beginning I tried a method using pallets as garden beds, it did work and I used them for several years.

Pallets are a good starting point for those with limited funds because pallets can be found for free many times.

Another great option are buckets with holes drilled in them for container gardens, I used pickle buckets from Firehouse Subs which only cost $2 each and cat litter buckets that would otherwise be recycled.

A few years in I tried deep mulch, I layered cardboard and topped it with deep mulch to supress weeds to make keeping up with the garden easy.

I topped the mulch off every now and then and it worked well.

Using very being pots I found on the marketplace I was able to increase the amount I could grow.

Using garden fabric around the pots helped.

My garden arch has Sweet Peas growing on it, they were propegated from a plant I got from my late friends garden.
